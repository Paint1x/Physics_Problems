# Calculus: Finding Area Under a Curve

### Definitions
- **Definite Integral**: Represents the area under the curve between two points.
- **Antiderivative**: The inverse of a derivative. For $\sin(x)$, it is $-\cos(x)$.

### The Problem
Calculate the area under $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $x = 0$ to $x = \pi$:

$$
\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) \, dx
$$

---

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Find the antiderivative of $\sin(x)$:**

$$
F(x) = -\cos(x)
$$

**2. Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:**

$$
\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) \, dx = [-\cos(x)]_{0}^{\pi}
$$

**3. Substitute the upper and lower limits:**

$$
(-\cos(\pi)) - (-\cos(0))
$$

**4. Evaluate the trigonometric terms:**
- $\cos(\pi) = -1$
- $\cos(0) = 1$

$$
(-(-1)) - (-1)
$$

**5. Final calculation:**

$$
1 + 1 = 2
$$

### Conclusion
The total area under the sine curve on this interval is:

$$
2
$$
