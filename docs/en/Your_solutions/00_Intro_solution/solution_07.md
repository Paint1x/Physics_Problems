# Logic & Series: The Fly and the Bicycle Problem

### Definitions & Concepts
- **Constant Speed Formula**: Distance = Speed × Time ($d = v \cdot t$).
- **The Insight**: The total distance the fly travels depends only on its speed and the total time it is moving. The fly stops moving when the bicycle hits the wall.

### Problem Parameters
- Distance to wall: $10\text{ m}$
- Bicycle speed: $1\text{ m/s}$
- Fly speed: $2\text{ m/s}$

---

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Find the time it takes for the bicycle to hit the wall:**

$$
t = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{10\text{ m}}{1\text{ m/s}} = 10\text{ s}
$$

**2. Identify the fly's total flight time:**
Since the fly flies until the bicycle hits the wall, the fly's time is also:

$$
t_{fly} = 10\text{ s}
$$

**3. Calculate total distance for the fly:**

$$
d_{fly} = v_{fly} \cdot t_{fly}
$$

$$
d_{fly} = 2\text{ m/s} \cdot 10\text{ s} = 20\text{ m}
$$

### Final Result
The fly travels a total distance of:

$$
20\text{ meters}
$$
