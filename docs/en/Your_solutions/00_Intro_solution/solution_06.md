# Function Analysis: Finding Local Extrema

### Definitions
- **Local Minimum**: The lowest point in a particular region (vertex of an upward parabola).
- **Vertex Formula**: The x-coordinate is found via $x = -b / 2a$.

### The Function

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

---

### Step-by-Step Analysis

**1. Determine the direction of the parabola:**
The leading coefficient $a = 3$. Since $3 > 0$, the parabola opens upward, indicating a **local minimum**.

**2. Calculate the $x$-coordinate of the vertex:**

$$
x = -\frac{b}{2a} = -\frac{-12}{2(3)}
$$

$$
x = \frac{12}{6} = 2
$$

**3. Calculate the $y$-coordinate by evaluating $f(2)$:**

$$
f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = 12 - 24 + 7 = -5
$$

### Conclusion
The function has a local minimum at:

$$
(2, -5)
$$
