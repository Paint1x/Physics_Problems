# Proportionality in Universal Law of Gravitation

### The Formula
The Universal Law of Gravitation is expressed as:

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

### Problem Statement
Determine the factor by which the force $F$ changes if:
- Distance $r$ is **doubled** ($r \to 2r$)
- Both masses $m_1$ and $m_2$ are **halved** ($m \to \frac{1}{2}m$)

---

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Express the new variables in terms of the old ones:**

$$
m_{1}' = \frac{1}{2}m_1, \quad m_{2}' = \frac{1}{2}m_2, \quad r' = 2r
$$

**2. Substitute these into the gravitational formula:**

$$
F_{new} = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}
$$

**3. Simplify the numerical coefficients:**
- In the numerator: $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}$
- In the denominator: $(2)^2 = 4$

$$
F_{new} = \frac{1/4}{4} \times \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)
$$

**4. Calculate the final factor:**

$$
\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{16}
$$

### Conclusion
The new force is **1/16** of the original force.

$$
F_{new} = \frac{1}{16} F
$$
