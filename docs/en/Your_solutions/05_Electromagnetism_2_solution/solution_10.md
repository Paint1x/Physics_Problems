# 10. Speed of Light in Media

**The Simple Explanation:**
The "speed of light" ($3 \times 10^8$ m/s) is only its speed in an empty vacuum. When light travels through physical stuff (like a diamond), it slows down. The "index of refraction" ($n$) is just a number that tells us how much it slows down.

**The Setup:**
* Speed of light in vacuum ($c$) = $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
* Diamond's index ($n$) = $2.42$

**Step-by-Step Math:**
To find the new speed ($v$), we just divide the vacuum speed by the diamond's index.

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

Plug in the numbers:

$$
v = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

**Final Answer:**

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$
