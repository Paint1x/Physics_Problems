# 5. Energy Stored by charge in a capacitor

**The Simple Explanation:**
A capacitor is like a water tank made of two metal plates. We are figuring out how big the tank is, how much energy is inside, how strong the field is between the plates, and how hard the plates are pulling toward each other.

**The Setup:**
* Area ($S$) = $0.02 \text{ m}^2$
* Distance ($d$) = $5 \text{ mm} = 0.005 \text{ m}$
* Voltage ($U$) = $500 \text{ V}$
* Constant ($\epsilon_0$) = $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$

**Step-by-Step Math:**

**1. Capacitance ($C$):** How much charge it can hold.

$$
C = \frac{\epsilon_0 \cdot S}{d}
$$

$$
C = \frac{8.854 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 0.02}{0.005} = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \text{ F}
$$

**2. Energy ($W$):** We use $W$ here to avoid confusing energy with Voltage $U$.

$$
W = \frac{1}{2} \cdot C \cdot U^2
$$

$$
W = 0.5 \cdot (3.54 \times 10^{-11}) \cdot (500)^2 = 4.425 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}
$$

**3. Electric Field ($E$):** Voltage divided by the distance between plates.

$$
E = \frac{U}{d}
$$

$$
E = \frac{500}{0.005} = 100,000 \text{ V/m}
$$

**4. Force of Attraction ($F$):**

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \epsilon_0 \cdot E^2 \cdot S
$$

$$
F = 0.5 \cdot (8.854 \times 10^{-12}) \cdot (100,000)^2 \cdot 0.02 = 8.854 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}
$$
