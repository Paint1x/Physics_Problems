# 7. Wavelength and Frequency

**The Simple Explanation:**
Light is a wave. If we know the length of the wave and how fast it travels (the speed of light), we can figure out how frequently those waves hit our eye.

**The Setup:**
* Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$
* Speed of light ($c$) = $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

**Step-by-Step Math:**

**1. Color Identification:**
$550 \text{ nm}$ lands right in the middle of the visible spectrum. This corresponds to the color **Yellow-Green** (which is why high-visibility safety vests use this color—our eyes are most sensitive to it!).

**2. Frequency ($f$):**

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

$$
f = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{550 \times 10^{-9}}
$$

**Final Answer:**

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$
