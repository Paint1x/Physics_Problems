# 9. Refraction (Snell's Law)

**The Simple Explanation:**
When light hits a thicker material (like going from air to glass), it gets bogged down and bends inward. Snell's law is the formula that tells us exactly how much the light will bend.

**The Setup:**
* Material 1 (Air) index ($n_1$) = $1.00$
* Angle 1 ($\theta_1$) = $30^\circ$
* Material 2 (Glass) index ($n_2$) = $1.50$
* Angle 2 ($\theta_2$) = What we need to find.

**Step-by-Step Math:**

$$
n_1 \cdot \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)
$$

Plug in the numbers:

$$
1.00 \cdot \sin(30^\circ) = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)
$$

We know $\sin(30^\circ)$ is $0.5$:

$$
0.5 = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)
$$

Divide both sides by $1.50$:

$$
\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.50} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333
$$

Use the inverse sine function (arcsin) on a calculator to find the angle:

$$
\theta_2 = \arcsin(0.333)
$$

**Final Answer:**

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.47^\circ
$$
