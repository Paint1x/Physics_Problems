# 1. Gauss's Law

**The Simple Explanation:**
Gauss's law tells us that the total amount of electric "wind" (flux) passing through a closed bubble (like our sphere) only depends on the amount of charge trapped inside it. The size of the bubble (radius) actually doesn't matter at all!

**The Setup:**
* Charge ($Q$) = $2 \text{ C}$
* The constant of empty space ($\epsilon_0$) = $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$

**Step-by-Step Math:**
To find the flux ($\Phi$), we divide the trapped charge by the constant of empty space.

$$
\Phi = \frac{Q}{\epsilon_0}
$$

Plug in the numbers:

$$
\Phi = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

**Final Answer:**

$$
\Phi \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N m}^2/\text{C}
$$
