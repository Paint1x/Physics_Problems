# 11. Power & Energy

**The Simple Explanation:**
First, we calculate how fast the resistor is burning electricity, which is called Power. Then, we multiply that "burn rate" by the total number of seconds it was running to get the total Energy consumed.

**The Setup:**
* Resistance ($R$) = $100 \, \Omega$
* Voltage ($V$) = $50 \text{ V}$
* Time ($t$) = $5 \text{ minutes} = 300 \text{ seconds}$

**Step-by-Step Math:**

**1. Power ($P$):**

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

$$
P = \frac{50^2}{100} = \frac{2500}{100} = 25 \text{ W}
$$

**2. Energy ($E$):**

$$
E = P \cdot t
$$

$$
E = 25 \cdot 300 = 7500 \text{ J}
$$
