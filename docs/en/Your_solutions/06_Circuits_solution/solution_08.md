# 8. AC Voltage Equation

**The Simple Explanation:**
Ohm's Law says Voltage equals Current times Resistance. Even though the current is waving up and down over time (AC), the rule still applies perfectly at every single second!

**The Setup:**
* Current ($I(t)$) = $2 \sin(120\pi t)$
* Resistance ($R$) = $50 \, \Omega$

**Step-by-Step Math:**
Use Ohm's Law:

$$
V(t) = I(t) \cdot R
$$

Plug in our given values:

$$
V(t) = [2 \sin(120\pi t)] \cdot 50
$$

Multiply the constants (2 and 50):

$$
V(t) = 100 \sin(120\pi t) \text{ V}
$$
