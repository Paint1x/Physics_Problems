# 7. Capacitors in Parallel

**The Simple Explanation:**
When capacitors are in parallel, they act like side-by-side buckets. To find the total storage size, we just add them together. Once we have the total size, we multiply it by the battery's push (voltage) to find the total charge, and use a standard formula for energy.

**The Setup:**
* Capacitor 1 ($C_1$) = $4 \, \mu\text{F} = 4 \times 10^{-6} \text{ F}$
* Capacitor 2 ($C_2$) = $6 \, \mu\text{F} = 6 \times 10^{-6} \text{ F}$
* Voltage ($V$) = $10 \text{ V}$

**Step-by-Step Math:**

**1. Total Capacitance ($C_{total}$):**

$$
C_{total} = C_1 + C_2 = 4 + 6 = 10 \, \mu\text{F}
$$

**2. Total Charge ($Q$):**

$$
Q = C_{total} \cdot V
$$

$$
Q = 10 \times 10^{-6} \cdot 10 = 100 \times 10^{-6} \text{ C} \text{ (or } 100 \, \mu\text{C})
$$

**3. Total Energy ($E$):**

$$
E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot C_{total} \cdot V^2
$$

$$
E = 0.5 \cdot (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot (10)^2
$$

$$
E = 0.5 \cdot (10 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot 100 = 500 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J} \text{ (or } 500 \, \mu\text{J})
$$
