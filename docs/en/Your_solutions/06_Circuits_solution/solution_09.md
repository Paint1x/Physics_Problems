# 9. Current

**The Simple Explanation:**
Current is the "rate of change" of charge. Whenever we need a rate of change from an equation, we use the calculus Power Rule to find the derivative!

**The Setup:**
* Charge Equation: $Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$
* Target time ($t$) = $3 \text{ s}$

**Step-by-Step Math:**
First, take the derivative of $Q(t)$ to find the current equation $I(t)$:
(The $5t^2$ becomes $10t$, and the plain $5$ turns into $0$).

$$
I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt} = 10t
$$

Now, plug in $t = 3$:

$$
I(3) = 10 \cdot 3 = 30 \text{ A}
$$
