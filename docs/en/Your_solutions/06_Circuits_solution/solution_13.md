# 13. Transformer Ratio

**The Simple Explanation:**
We want to drop the voltage from 120 down to 9. A transformer uses loops of wire (turns) to change voltage. We just need to figure out exactly how many turns the second coil needs to match that exact same voltage drop ratio.

**The Setup:**
* Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $120 \text{ V}$
* Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = $9.0 \text{ V}$
* Primary turns ($N_p$) = $400$

**Step-by-Step Math:**

$$
\frac{N_s}{N_p} = \frac{V_s}{V_p}
$$

Multiply both sides by $N_p$ to get $N_s$ by itself:

$$
N_s = N_p \cdot \left(\frac{V_s}{V_p}\right)
$$

$$
N_s = 400 \cdot \left(\frac{9}{120}\right)
$$

$$
N_s = 400 \cdot 0.075 = 30 \text{ turns}
$$
