
# River Crossing: Relative Velocity Analysis

## 1. Problem Parameters
- River Velocity ($v_r$): $2 \text{ m/s}$ East
- Boat Speed ($v_{br}$): $5 \text{ m/s}$
- River Width ($w$): $200 \text{ m}$
- Desired Path: Directly North

---

## 2. Calculating the Heading Angle
To counter the river's flow, the boat must head at an angle $\theta$ West of North.

$$
\sin(\theta) = \frac{v_{river}}{v_{boat}} = \frac{2}{5}
$$

$$
\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58^\circ
$$

**Heading:** $23.58^\circ$ West of North.

---

## 3. Calculating the Resultant Speed ($v_N$)
The actual speed of the boat relative to the ground in the North direction is:

$$
v_N = \sqrt{v_{boat}^2 - v_{river}^2}
$$

$$
v_N = \sqrt{5^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## 4. Time of Flight
The time taken to cross the $200 \text{ m}$ wide river:

$$
t = \frac{\text{Width}}{v_N} = \frac{200}{4.58} \approx 43.67 \text{ s}
$$
