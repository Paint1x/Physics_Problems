# Physics Problem: Comparing Momentum

## 1. Variables and Conversions
To calculate momentum ($p = m \cdot v$), we must use kilograms ($kg$) for mass.

- **Fly mass ($m_1$):** $2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
- **Fly velocity ($v_1$):** $10 \text{ m/s}$
- **Tennis ball mass ($m_2$):** $60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$
- **Tennis ball velocity ($v_2$):** $1 \text{ m/s}$

---

## 2. Momentum Calculations

**Momentum of the Fly:**

$$
p_1 = 0.002 \text{ kg} \times 10 \text{ m/s} = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

**Momentum of the Tennis Ball:**

$$
p_2 = 0.06 \text{ kg} \times 1 \text{ m/s} = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

---

## 3. Final Comparison
Since $0.06 > 0.02$, the tennis ball has a greater momentum than the fly.

**Result:** The **60-gram tennis ball** has more momentum.
